Advice for Incoming Students:

  • Get the lowest dining plan

    • Unless you plan on eating at the dining hall for every meal, ther's no point in getting anything other than the lowest meal plan. Options at the dining hall are limited, and you'll get bored quick. If you do happen to need more money for the dining hall, you can add additional funds throughout the quarter. Remember, you can put money into your account but you can't take money out.

  • Be prepared to walk a lot

    • Ever since I got to UW, I've been getting over 7,000 steps every day. Your schedule will most likely have you scrambling all over campus, so my advice is to wear comfortable clothes and a good pair of shoes. Keeping a water bottle on you is also comes in handy.

  • Step outside your comfort zone

    • Especially for out-of-state students, I urge you to step outside of your comfort zone. Joining clubs, participating in school events, and even just starting a conversation with the person next to you, will help you build your connections and explore more opportunities. Even if you're quieter person, chances are the person next to you is also looking to find a familar face at school.
